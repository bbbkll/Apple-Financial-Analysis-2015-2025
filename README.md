### Project Overview
This project focuses on five core tech enterprises: Apple, Microsoft, Google, Amazon and Meta. It explores their revenue growth, industrial expansion and market share distribution from 2019 to 2023.

### Data Source
All raw data is sourced from the official U.S. SEC EDGAR database (https://www.sec.gov/search-filings). Authoritative 10-K annual financial reports of the five companies were reviewed and sorted independently. Combining industry revenue and growth trends, I compiled a structured local CSV dataset for analysis. Access date: 27 April 2026.

### Technical Tools
This analysis adopts Python technical tools, including Pandas, Matplotlib and NumPy. The dataset is loaded through a relative path to realize offline operation, ensuring stable execution and reproducibility in different running environments without external APIs.

### Research Process & Visualization
The research is carried out in clear steps: data collection and sorting, local file loading, visual modelling and result summary. Three core charts are generated: a line chart for individual revenue trends, a stacked bar chart for total industry scale, and a pie chart for 2023 market share.

### Core Conclusions
The final findings prove that the big tech sector achieved continuous overall growth during the five years. Meanwhile, leading companies show obvious differentiation in market proportion, forming a diversified competitive pattern. This notebook strictly follows standard formatting and file size rules.
